My Research

"The best way to predict the future is to invent it." — Alan Kay

April, 2023 - May,2024
Supervisor-Dr. Nur Mohammad

Optimizing Neural Network Performance for Short-Term Load Forecasting Using Time and Source Lag Features

A Bidirectional Gated Recurrent Unit (Bi-GRU) model optimized by Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) was developed for short-term load forecasting using hourly data from the Power Grid Company of Bangladesh (PGCB) from January 2016 to March 2023. Advanced feature engineering, including time lag and source lag features, captured complex dependencies in the data. The PSO-tuned Bi-GRU model achieved a MAPE of 2.49, significantly outperforming traditional models.

August, 2024 - October, 2024
Supervisor-Dr. Nursadul Mamun

Emo-Former: Text-Independent Speech Emotion Recognition using Hybrid Transformer CNN Model.

EmoFormer, a hybrid model combining Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and Transformer encoders, was developed for Speech Emotion Recognition (SER) to improve machine understanding of human emotions. Trained on the Expressive Anechoic Recordings of Speech (EARS) dataset, EmoFormer used MFCCs and x-vectors for feature extraction. The model achieved 90% accuracy in recognizing five emotions and was successfully scaled to 23 emotion categories, demonstrating its robustness across varying levels of complexity.

June, 2024 - October 2024
Supervisor-Dr. Nursadul Mamun

A Comprehensive Review of Emotion Recognition Techniques: From Unimodal Ap proaches to Multimodal Fusion Paradigms.

This study reviews unimodal and multimodal emotion recognition using datasets like RAVDESS, EMO-DB, and DEAP. It highlights the benefits of combining audio, visual, and physiological signals to improve emotion detection. The review also covers preprocessing techniques and concludes that advanced deep learning models, including CNNs, RNNs, and multimodal networks, are key to capturing complex emotional patterns and improving accuracy.
